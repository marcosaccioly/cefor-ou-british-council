What key regulatory and policy requirements relating to TNE in the overseas partner country are relevant to this project, and how have these been considered within the project design?

The project recognises that transnational education activity in Brazil operates within a regulated higher education environment overseen by the Ministry of Education (MEC). The sector is governed by the National Education Guidelines and Framework Law (LDB – Law No. 9.394/1996), Decree No. 12.456/2025, which established the new National Distance Education Policy, and complementary regulations issued by the Ministry of Education (MEC) and the National Education Council (CNE). Together, these regulations establish requirements related to institutional accreditation, quality assurance, curriculum organisation, and the provision of face-to-face, blended, and distance education programmes.Key considerations include institutional accreditation requirements, programme approval processes, quality assurance expectations, and regulations relating to online and distance education provision.

As the project focuses on knowledge exchange, institutional capacity building, and the identification of future opportunities rather than the delivery of a TNE programme, these requirements have been considered through the inclusion of institutional stakeholders, the review of relevant regulatory frameworks, and the development of recommendations that align with national policies and quality standards.

The previous participation of Ifes in international academic projects and partnerships and, specifically within the context of Cefor, in the EMBRACE project (Erasmus+ Capacity Building in Higher Education), demonstrates its capacity to engage in international cooperation initiatives in accordance with the Brazilian higher education regulatory framework. Furthermore, IFES was one of the institutions selected under the CAPES Global.edu programme, as part of a consortium with other Brazilian institutions, through a proposal for a network called Rede GLOBALIS – International Cooperation for Sustainable Development

Analysis - what are the key gender equality issues that are relevant to your project and how it will contribute to addressing these? (200 words max)

Brazil presents significant gender inequalities in access to higher education, particularly for women of working age. Women perform on average 3.4 times more unpaid domestic and care work than men (IBGE, 2022), limiting their participation in face-to-face education. Distance learning is a critical pathway for these women: the Brazil Distance Learning Census 2023 shows they account for more than 60% of enrolments nationwide, including working women, mothers and women in remote regions. Teenage pregnancy also remains a relevant factor in school dropout, with distance learning offering a flexible qualification alternative. At Ifes/Cefor, the Corte de Lovelace project — led by a woman — works on attracting and retaining women in computing, robotics and technology, a historically male-dominated field, serving as a concrete example of distance learning as an instrument of gender equity. Ifes campuses host Gender and Sexuality Studies and Research Nuclei (Nepgens) across the network, developing actions focused on gender equity and sexual diversity, demonstrating the institution's structural commitment to these issues. Both institutions will seek gender balance in the selection of participants across all project activities.
