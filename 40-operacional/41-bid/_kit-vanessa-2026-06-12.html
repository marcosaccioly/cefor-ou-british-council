Risk Management — Seção 5 obrigatória · está vazia
O formulário exige no mínimo 1 risco (máx. 5). Três prontos abaixo — cada um com o tipo (dropdown), probabilidade/impacto sugeridos, e os dois textos (descrição + mitigação).

Risco 1 — Tipo: Partnership / Collaboration · Probabilidade: Low (Unlikely) · Impacto: Moderate

Descrição (150 palavras máx.):
North–South international partnerships can reproduce asymmetries, with the UK partner's agenda or resources dominating decisions, expectations diverging, or workload falling unevenly. If unmanaged, this could weaken ownership on the Ifes side and undermine the equitable partnership on which the project is built.
Mitigação (150 palavras máx.):
The project is designed as a genuine co-design: roles, responsibilities, leadership of activities and authorship of outputs are shared from the outset. A joint Steering Committee co-chaired by both institutions takes strategic decisions by consensus, meeting fortnightly with a shared decision log, and each institution leads the activities aligned to its strengths. The partner lead (Vanessa Battestin, Ifes/Cefor) monitors balance and escalates any imbalance to the Committee.

Risco 2 — Tipo: Operational / Delivery · Probabilidade: Medium (Possible) · Impacto: Minor

Descrição:
Delays may arise from the availability of staff across multiple Ifes campuses, competing institutional commitments, or the scheduling of the campus consultation and the OU visit within the project timeline, potentially slowing the diagnostic, mapping or co-design activities.
Mitigação:
The work plan includes time buffers and sequences activities so that a delay in one does not block the others. A focal point at each participating campus coordinates local input, and fortnightly co-coordination meetings track progress against milestones. The OU visit and workshops are scheduled early and confirmed well in advance. The co-coordinators (one per institution) are responsible for monitoring delivery.

Risco 3 — Tipo: Health & Safety · Probabilidade: Low (Unlikely) · Impacto: Moderate

Descrição:
The project includes one international visit by Ifes/Cefor representatives to The Open University. Visa delays, travel disruption, illness or other health-and-safety issues during travel could prevent or postpone the visit, affecting relationship-building and the co-design of the framework and roadmap.
Mitigação:
Travel will be planned well in advance to allow time for visas and arrangements, following the travel and health-and-safety policies of both institutions, including appropriate travel and health insurance. Should travel be prevented, the core objectives of the visit can be partly met through intensive online sessions. The Ifes/Cefor lead is responsible for monitoring and coordinating travel readiness.

Joint governance arrangements Seção 1 pontua em Quality 35%
Campo vazio. Pede como a decisão estratégica e operacional é compartilhada entre OU e Ifes.
The project will be governed by a joint Steering Committee co-chaired by the Open University/IET and Ifes/Cefor leads, meeting online fortnightly to oversee progress, budget and risk. Strategic decisions — scope, dissemination, and the framework and roadmap — are taken jointly by both leads by consensus. Day-to-day operational decisions are delegated to two co-coordinators, one per institution, supported by a focal point at each participating Ifes campus. All key outputs are jointly reviewed and signed off by both institutions. A shared workspace and a simple decision log keep communication transparent, and any disagreement is escalated to the Steering Committee. This structure ensures balanced accountability and shared leadership across the UK and overseas partners throughout the project.

Ifes/Cefor strengthens its institutional capacity in distance learning — including instructional design, quality assurance, learner support, accessibility and AI in education — through structured knowledge exchange with the OU/IET, evidenced by staff participation and by the diagnostic and mapping reports informing future provision.

Why 200 palavras máx.
We want to make the partnership's learning visible and useful beyond the two institutions. Our goals are: to share practical insights on how high-quality, inclusive distance learning can widen access to regionally specialised expertise; to raise the profile of the OU–Ifes collaboration and attract future partners and funders; to demonstrate to the British Council and the wider sector the value of equitable UK–Brazil transnational education; and to keep both institutional communities — and the participating Ifes campuses — informed and engaged, so the collaboration is owned internally and sustained after the project ends.
What 200 palavras máx.
We will disseminate: the joint framework and strategic roadmap for future online and transnational education collaboration; the institutional mapping report, with gender-disaggregated data; the comparative capability diagnostic; knowledge-exchange workshop materials and recordings; lessons learned on inclusive distance learning and on building an equitable North–South partnership; and at least one jointly authored paper or conference presentation. All outputs will be open access.
To whom 200 palavras máx.
Primary audiences: leadership and staff at OU/IET and Ifes/Cefor, and the participating Ifes campuses across Espírito Santo. Wider audiences: the British Council and the Going Global Partnerships community; the Brazilian Federal Network (Conif, 41 institutions); distance-learning and educational-technology researchers and practitioners in the UK and Brazil; and policy and institutional stakeholders interested in inclusive distance learning, gender equity and transnational education. Specific gender and inclusion insights will also be shared with the Ifes Nepgens network.
How 400 palavras máx.
Dissemination will use channels appropriate to each audience. Internally, findings will be shared through reports to the leadership of both institutions and through knowledge-sharing sessions within IET and Cefor; the Community of Practice will act as a continuous channel for exchange between teams and campuses. Externally, we will publish the framework, roadmap and mapping report as open-access documents on both institutions' platforms and through a dedicated project web page. We will submit at least one jointly authored paper to a peer-reviewed journal and/or present at an international conference on distance education, educational technology or internationalisation. Shorter updates and results will be shared via the institutions' websites and social media channels, in English and Portuguese, to reach audiences in both countries. Gender and inclusion insights will be shared with the Nepgens network and incorporated into relevant institutional reporting. Where useful, we will offer a public-facing webinar to share findings with the wider Ifes network and interested partners.
When 200 palavras máx.
Communication runs throughout the project and beyond. During delivery, we will share brief progress updates at key milestones — after the diagnostic, the campus mapping, the OU visit, and the publication of the framework and roadmap. Internal reporting to leadership follows the interim and final reporting points. Open-access outputs are published as they are completed, from mid-project onwards. The paper and/or conference presentation will be submitted towards the end of the project and after completion. The Community of Practice maintains ongoing, informal communication after the funded period ends.
